# Stable Luminescent Radicals and Radical-Based LEDs with Doublet Emission

## Cite_Key
cui2020stable

## Problem
Conventional closed-shell OLEDs are limited by spin-statistics, allowing only 25% of electrically generated excitons (singlets) to decay radiatively.

## Method
Review of stable luminescent radicals (TTM, PTM, BTM) and doublet emission mechanisms. Discusses molecular design principles for stability and high photoluminescence quantum efficiency.

## Data
Literature review of luminescent radicals and OLED device performance.

## Metrics
External quantum efficiency (EQE), photoluminescence quantum efficiency (PLQE), emission wavelength.

## Findings
Luminescent radicals allow doublet emission, which is completely spin-allowed, circumventing the spin-statistic limit and allowing internal quantum efficiency (IQE) up to 100%. Donor-acceptor type radicals show higher emission efficiency.

## Limitations
Most doublet-emission OLEDs are currently confined to a few types of radicals (TTM, PTM, BTM) and often exhibit deep-red to near-infrared emission.
