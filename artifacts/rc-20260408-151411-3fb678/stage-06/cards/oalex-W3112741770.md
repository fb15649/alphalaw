# Precise Regulation of Distance between Associated Pyrene Units and Control of Emission Energy and Kinetics in Solid State

## Cite_Key
wang2020precise

## Problem
Precise regulation of distances in dimers to tune emission behaviors and energy kinetics of organic luminogens in the solid state is challenging.

## Method
Designed four pyrene derivatives using xanthene as a template frame to precisely regulate the distance between pyrene units. Performed qualitative and quantitative analysis of π–π interactions in crystals.

## Data
Crystals of pyrene derivatives (X1P, X2P, X1EP, X2EP).

## Metrics
Emission wavelength (nm), excimer decay time (τE, ns), distance between pyrene units (Å), PL quantum yield (PLQY).

## Findings
Short distance and strong π–π interactions are vital to lower excited-state energy and weaken delayed fluorescence. X2P crystal exhibits deep-blue excimer emission (424 nm) with a shorter decay time (26.4 ns) compared to X1P crystal (475 nm, 106.0 ns).

## Limitations
The study primarily focuses on pyrene derivatives and the specific xanthene-based molecular frame.
