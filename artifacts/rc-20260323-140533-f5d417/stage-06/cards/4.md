# The mass-energy equivalence and length contraction are consistent with compression of a fluidic aether with a speed of sound equal to the speed of light

## Cite_Key
johansson2020massenergy

## Problem
Explaining relativistic effects (Lorentz factor, mass-energy equivalence) through mechanical medium properties rather than spacetime geometry

## Method
Fluid dynamics analysis with speed of sound equal to speed of light; compression energy calculations; analysis of particle accelerator data

## Data
Relativistic particle accelerator results; Lorentz transformation data

## Metrics
Lorentz factor derivation from fluid dynamics; mass-energy equivalence from compression energy

## Findings
Lorentz factor emerges naturally when approaching speed of sound in fluidic medium; E=mc² identical to compression energy of fluid with c as sound speed; particle accelerators may detect fluidic aether effects

## Limitations
Preprint without peer review; zero citations; purely theoretical without novel predictions
