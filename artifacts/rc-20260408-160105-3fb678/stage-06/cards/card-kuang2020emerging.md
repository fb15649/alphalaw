# Emerging Chiral Materials

## Cite_Key
kuang2020emerging

## Problem
Comprehensively reviewing the state-of-the-art in chiral nanomaterials, including synthesis of high chiral purity nanostructures, chiral assemblies, origin of symmetry breaking, theory calculations, and applications.

## Method
Editorial review synthesizing advances across five key aspects: synthesis of functional chiral materials, assemblies and reconfigurable chirality, origin of symmetry breaking, theory calculations, and applications.

## Data
Examples spanning polymers, semiconductors, metals, supramolecular 2D materials, metal-organic frameworks, liquid crystals, plasmonic nanostructures, and carbon nanotubes with chiroptical properties.

## Metrics
Chiral purity/enantiomeric yield; circular dichroism signals; chiroptical dissymmetry factors; circularly polarized luminescence performance.

## Findings
Chirality can be transferred from sub-nanometer biochemical molecules through nanoscale to sub-micrometer metastructures. Key challenges include: (1) fabricating complex chiral structures with high enantiomeric purity, (2) scalable manufacturing, (3) understanding chirality transfer across scales (ångström to macroscopic), (4) fast modulation of circular polarization for quantum computing, and (5) manipulating spins/electrons/photons with multi-degrees of freedom.

## Limitations
Review/editorial format; no new experimental data; forward-looking challenges require extensive future research.

## Citation
Kuang, H., Xu, C., & Tang, Z. (2020). Advanced Materials. doi:10.1002/adma.202005110
