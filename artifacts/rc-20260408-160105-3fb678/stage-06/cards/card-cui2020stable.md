# Stable Luminescent Radicals and Radical-Based LEDs with Doublet Emission

## Cite_Key
cui2020stable

## Problem
Overcoming the spin-statistical limit of conventional OLEDs (25% singlet excitons) by utilizing luminescent radicals with doublet emission where spin-forbidden transitions are eliminated.

## Method
Review of stable luminescent radical design (TTM, PTM, BTM families) and doublet-emission OLED fabrication; analysis of steric and delocalization effects on radical stability; DFT calculations of electronic structure.

## Data
PLQE values for radical emitters (0.8% for pure TTM to 86% for PTM-3NCz); OLED EQE values (2.4% to 27%); photostability half-times; electroluminescence spectra.

## Metrics
External quantum efficiency (EQE); photoluminescence quantum efficiency (PLQE); emission wavelength; photostability (t1/2); CIE coordinates.

## Findings
Doublet emission from monoradicals eliminates spin-forbidden transitions, enabling 100% IQE. Donor-acceptor radical design lifts HOMO-SOMO/SOMO-LUMO energy degeneracy, increasing transition dipole moment ~20-fold. PTM-3NCz with non-Aufbau electronic structure achieved PLQE of 86% and EQE of 27%. Asymmetric molecular geometry favors higher PLQE than symmetric structures.

## Limitations
Deep-red/NIR emission range limited; long-term device stability challenges; limited to triarylmethyl radical families; photostability varies significantly among radical types.

## Citation
