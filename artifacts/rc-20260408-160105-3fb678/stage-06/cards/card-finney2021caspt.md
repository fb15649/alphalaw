# CASPT2 molecular geometries of Fe(II) spin-crossover complexes

## Cite_Key
finney2021caspt

## Problem
Precisely determining molecular geometry of Fe(II) spin-crossover complexes and understanding the relationship between geometric structure and magnetic spin state.

## Method
CASPT2 quantum chemical calculations with various active spaces and basis sets to optimize geometries of Fe(II) spin-crossover complexes and analyze geometry-spin state relationships.

## Data
Optimized molecular geometries of Fe(II) complexes; active space and basis set effects; spin-state energetics.

## Metrics
Geometry optimization accuracy; spin-state energy gaps; active space convergence; basis set effects.

## Findings
Molecular geometry optimization reveals clear relationships between geometric structure and magnetic spin state in Fe(II) complexes. Active space and basis set choices significantly affect geometry determination and spin-state predictions.

## Limitations
Computational cost of CASPT2 limits system size; accuracy depends on active space selection; experimental validation needed.

## Citation
Finney, B.A., Roy Chowdhury, S., Kirkvold, C., & Vlaisavljevich, B. (2021). PCCP. doi:10.1039/d1cp04885f
