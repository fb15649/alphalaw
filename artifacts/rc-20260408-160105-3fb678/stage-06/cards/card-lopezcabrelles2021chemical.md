# Chemical design and magnetic ordering in thin layers of 2D MOFs

## Cite_Key
lopezcabrelles2021chemical

## Problem
Determining how crystal structure and symmetry control magnetic ordering and critical temperatures in layered metal-organic frameworks.

## Method
Chemical design of 2D MOFs through systematic variation of metal nodes and organic ligands to control crystal symmetry and measure resulting magnetic properties.

## Data
Crystal structures of layered MOFs; magnetic ordering temperatures; symmetry classifications.

## Metrics
Critical temperatures (Tc); magnetic ordering parameters; crystal symmetry group classifications.

## Findings
Crystal structure and symmetry, controlled through metal node and ligand choice, directly determine magnetic ordering and critical temperatures in layered MOFs. Symmetry classes can be mapped to specific magnetic property outcomes.

## Limitations
Study focused on thin-layer 2D MOFs; 3D bulk behavior may differ; limited to specific metal-ligand combinations.
