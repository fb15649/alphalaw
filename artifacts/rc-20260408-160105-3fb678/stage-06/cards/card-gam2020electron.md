# Electron counting and bonding patterns in assemblies of three and more silver-rich superatoms

## Cite_Key
gam2020electron

## Problem
Understanding stable electron counts and bonding patterns in condensed superatom assemblies (vertex-sharing, face-sharing, interpenetration of icosahedral superatoms) and how local atomic geometry determines electronic properties.

## Method
Electron counting analysis and bonding pattern characterization of silver-rich superatom assemblies, drawing analogies to closed-shell molecules obeying the octet rule.

## Data
Icosahedral superatom condensations with various sharing modes (vertex-sharing, face-sharing, interpenetration); stable electron count configurations.

## Metrics
Stable electron counts; bonding pattern classifications; superatom assembly geometries.

## Findings
Icosahedral superatom assemblies exhibit stable electron counts analogous to closed-shell molecular configurations. Different condensation modes (vertex-sharing, face-sharing, interpenetration) produce distinct electronic structures and stability patterns.

## Limitations
Focused on silver-rich superatoms; generalizability to other metal systems may be limited.

## Citation
Gam, F., Liu, C.W., Kahlal, S., & Saillard, J.Y. (2020). Nanoscale. doi:10.1039/d0nr05179a
