# Spatiotemporal molecular medicine: A new era of clinical and translational medicine

## Cite_Key
wang2021spatiotemporal

## Problem
Integrating spatial and temporal dimensions with molecular multi-omics to achieve four-dimensional understanding of disease pathogenesis, diagnosis, and therapy.

## Method
Conceptual framework proposing spatiotemporal molecular medicine as a new discipline integrating clinical spatialization (genetics, populations, individuals), temporalization (disease progression, therapy response), phenomics, and molecular multi-omics with AI/computational models.

## Data
Examples from spatial transcriptomics, spatial proteomics, single-cell RNA sequencing, in situ hybridization, and clinical imaging modalities (4D ultrasound, fluorescence endomicroscopy, CT).

## Metrics
Spatial resolution of transcriptomic/proteomic mapping; temporal dynamics of molecular changes; cell-cell interaction networks; organogenesis monitoring capabilities.

## Findings
Spatiotemporal molecular medicine provides 4D (3D space + time) understanding by integrating: spatial transcriptomics/proteomics for tissue-level molecular mapping, single-cell analysis for cell-cell communication, temporal dynamics for disease progression tracking, and AI models for prediction. Clinical spatialization operates at genetics, population, and individual organ levels. Clinical temporalization covers disease history, phenome dynamics, and therapy responses.

## Limitations
Conceptual framework without primary experimental data; significant technical challenges in measurement and analysis standardization; clinical implementation requires extensive validation and infrastructure development.

## Citation
