# Precise Regulation of Distance between Associated Pyrene Units and Control of Emission Energy and Kinetics in Solid State

## Cite_Key
wang2020precise

## Problem
Precisely controlling the distance between pyrene units in molecular dimers and understanding how this distance determines emission energy and decay kinetics in the solid state.

## Method
Design and synthesis of four pyrene derivatives (X1P, X2P, X1EP, X2EP) using xanthene as a rigid template frame to regulate π-π distances; single-crystal XRD, PL spectroscopy, NCI analysis, and EDA-FF energy decomposition.

## Data
Single-crystal structures with measured pyrene-pyrene distances (3.358–3.713 Å); PL spectra; excimer decay times (3.9–106.0 ns); PLQY values (13.1–50.5%); NCI and EDA-FF interaction energies.

## Metrics
Maximum emission wavelength (nm); excimer decay time τE (ns); PLQY (%); rate constants (kE, kr, kISC); electrostatic (EE), repulsion (ER), and dispersion (ED) energies from EDA-FF.

## Findings
X2P crystal with intramolecular excimer at 3.675 Å shows deep-blue emission at 424 nm and τE = 26.4 ns, while X1P with intermolecular excimer at 3.358 Å shows sky-blue emission at 475 nm with τE = 106.0 ns. Shorter distance and stronger π-π interactions lower excited-state energy and weaken delayed fluorescence. Quantitative NCI analysis showed dispersion energy ED = -92.38 kJ/mol for X1P vs -76.37 kJ/mol for X2P.

## Limitations
Limited to pyrene-xanthene system; rigid xanthene frame required for distance control; multiple crystal polymorphs (bulk vs needle for X2EP) complicate analysis.

## Citation
Wang, J., et al. (2020). CCS Chemistry. doi:10.31635/ccschem.020.202000556
