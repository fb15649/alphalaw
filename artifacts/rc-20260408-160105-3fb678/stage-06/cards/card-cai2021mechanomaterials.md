# Mechanomaterials: A Rational Deployment of Forces and Geometries in Programming Functional Materials

## Cite_Key
cai2021mechanomaterials

## Problem
Establishing design principles for programming functional materials by leveraging force-geometry-property relationships across multiple scales.

## Method
Review and synthesis of design principles for mechanomaterials across four scales (atomic, molecular, nanoscale, microscale), identifying self-similar force-geometry-property patterns.

## Data
Cross-scale material design examples; force-geometry-property relationships at atomic through microscale levels.

## Metrics
Functional property modulation; scale-dependent design parameters; force-geometry coupling efficiency.

## Findings
Functional materials can be rationally programmed by deploying forces and geometries across four scales (atomic, molecular, nanoscale, microscale). Self-similar patterns in force-geometry-property relationships enable predictive design across scales.

## Limitations
Framework is conceptual; quantitative cross-scale predictive models still developing; limited to specific material classes reviewed.

## Citation
Cai, P., Wang, C., Gao, H., & Chen, X. (2021). Advanced Materials. doi:10.1002/adma.202007977
