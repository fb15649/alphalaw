# Exploratory factor analysis and principal component analysis in clinical studies: Which one should you use?

## Cite_Key
alavi2020exploratory

## Problem
Clarifying the conceptual and statistical differences between exploratory factor analysis (EFA) and principal component analysis (PCA) to guide appropriate method selection in clinical research.

## Method
Methodological comparison of EFA and PCA through conceptual analysis, contrasting their objectives, model assumptions, variance decomposition approaches, and appropriate application scenarios with clinical examples (hospitalization stress vs. stressors).

## Data
Comparative framework of EFA vs PCA characteristics; example applications using hospitalization stress (EFA: latent factors of security and attachment) and hospitalization-related stressors (PCA: components of physical and psychosocial agents).

## Metrics
Factor loadings (threshold >0.4); communality; uniqueness; variance explained; factor/component retention criteria.

## Findings
EFA analyzes shared covariance to identify latent constructs (reflective structure), while PCA analyzes total variance for data reduction (formative structure). EFA is appropriate when latent traits underlie observed variables; PCA is appropriate for data reduction with weighted sums of indicators. Key distinctions: EFA factors are explanatory (not causal); PCA components may be challenging to interpret in high-dimensional data. Clinical knowledge should guide method selection regardless of statistical patterns found.

## Limitations
Editorial format without primary data analysis; simplified comparison may not cover all edge cases; assumes researchers have sufficient theoretical knowledge to choose appropriately.

## Citation
