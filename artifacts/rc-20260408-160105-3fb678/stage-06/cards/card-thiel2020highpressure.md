# High-Pressure Crystallography as a Guide in the Design of Single-Molecule Magnets

## Cite_Key
thiel2020highpressure

## Problem
Understanding how molecular geometry distortion affects magnetic anisotropy and zero-field splitting in single-molecule magnets.

## Method
Systematic high-pressure crystallographic distortion of molecular geometry combined with measurement of zero-field splitting parameter D to establish magneto-structural correlations.

## Data
High-pressure crystallographic data; zero-field splitting parameters; molecular geometry distortions under pressure.

## Metrics
Zero-field splitting parameter D; magnetic anisotropy measures; structural distortion parameters under pressure.

## Findings
Magnetic anisotropy in single-molecule magnets arises from the interplay between ligand field geometry and spin-orbit coupling. Systematic geometric distortion under pressure directly modulates the zero-field splitting parameter D.

## Limitations
High-pressure conditions may not directly translate to ambient-condition design strategies; limited molecular systems studied.

## Citation
Thiel, A.M., Damgaard-Møller, E., & Overgaard, J. (2020). Inorganic Chemistry. doi:10.1021/acs.inorgchem.9b02794
