# Light-matter coupling and quantum geometry in moiré materials

## Cite_Key
topp2021lightmatter

## Problem
Understanding how the quantum geometry of Bloch wave functions (quantum metric, Berry connection) governs light-matter coupling in flat-band moiré systems.

## Method
Theoretical analysis establishing fundamental relations between quantum geometric quantities (intraband quantum metric, interband Berry connection) and light-matter coupling in flat-band systems.

## Data
Quantum geometric quantities of Bloch wave functions; light-matter coupling parameters; flat-band system properties.

## Metrics
Quantum metric; Berry connection; light-matter coupling strength; band velocity contributions.

## Findings
Geometric contributions (intraband quantum metric, interband Berry connection) govern light-matter coupling even when band velocities vanish. Spatial geometry of wave functions determines electromagnetic response properties in flat-band systems.

## Limitations
Theoretical study requiring experimental validation; focused on specific moiré material systems.

## Citation
Topp, G.E., et al. (2021). Physical Review B. doi:10.1103/physrevb.104.064306
