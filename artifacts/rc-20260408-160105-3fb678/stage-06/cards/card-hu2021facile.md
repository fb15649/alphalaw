# Facile Access to Polar-Functionalized Ultrahigh Molecular Weight Polyethylene at Ambient Conditions

## Cite_Key
hu2021facile

## Problem
Producing polar-functionalized ultrahigh molecular weight polyethylene (UHMWPE, Mn > 10^6 g/mol) via copolymerization of ethylene with polar monomers at ambient conditions (1 bar, 30°C), which was previously extremely challenging.

## Method
Design of terphenyl-substituted α-diimine nickel catalysts (Ni1-Ni4) with concerted double-layer steric strategy; coordination-insertion copolymerization at 1 bar/30°C; DFT calculations for mechanistic insights; Sterimol B1 steric parameter analysis.

## Data
Polyethylene Mn values up to 6.04 × 10^6 g/mol at 1 bar; polar-functionalized UHMWPE with Mn = 0.83-1.10 × 10^6 g/mol; up to 122 polar units incorporated per chain; DFT-calculated energy barriers (10.4-18.7 kcal/mol).

## Metrics
Number-average molecular weight Mn (g/mol); catalytic activity (g mol^-1 h^-1); melting point Tm (°C); branches per 1000C; Sterimol B1 steric parameter; energy barriers from DFT.

## Findings
Well-designed α-diimine nickel catalysts produce UHMWPE (Mn = 6.04 × 10^6 g/mol) and polar-functionalized UHMWPE (Mn = 0.83-1.10 × 10^6 g/mol) at 1 bar/30°C—enhancing molecular weights by two orders of magnitude over previous reports. Sterimol B1 parameter quantitatively correlates with polymer molecular weight. DFT reveals low barrier (10.4 kcal/mol) for ethylene insertion as rate-limiting step enables high activity. The double-layer steric strategy (rigid planar phenyl + bulky substituents) inhibits chain transfer while facilitating ethylene coordination.

## Limitations
Catalyst synthesis requires multi-step procedures; branched polymer architecture (not perfectly linear); limited to specific polar monomers; long reaction times needed for highest molecular weights.
