# Mechanical properties of 3D printed macroscopic models of schwarzites

## Cite_Key
gaal2021mechanical

## Problem
Understanding whether mechanical behavior of schwarzites is governed by topological features that are scale-invariant across atomic and macroscopic scales.

## Method
3D printing of macroscopic schwarzite models and mechanical testing, comparing deformation mechanisms with atomic-scale simulations to identify scale-invariant topological effects.

## Data
Mechanical deformation data from macroscopic 3D-printed schwarzites; atomic-scale simulation data; topological feature analysis.

## Metrics
Mechanical properties (stiffness, strength, deformation modes); topological descriptors; scale-invariant correlation measures.

## Findings
Mechanical behavior of schwarzites is dominated by topological features that are scale-invariant—correlation between atomic-scale and macroscopic-scale deformation mechanisms was demonstrated, supporting self-similar patterns across scales.

## Limitations
3D printing resolution limits feature fidelity; material differences between macroscopic models and atomic-scale systems.

## Citation
Gaal, V., et al. (2021). Nano Select. doi:10.1002/nano.202100147
